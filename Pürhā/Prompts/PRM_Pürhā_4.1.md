An extreme macro photograph for a 1:1 square aspect ratio album cover. Extremely shallow depth of field, sharp focus only on a few millimeters. A single, taut acoustic guitar string, made of translucent warm ivory nylon, is tightly wrapped and knotted around a heavily corroded, cratered fragment of an old iron colonial cannon. The raking, low-angle twilight sun hits the side, carving the orange rust, dried-blood brown tones, and deep crevices of the metal like a miniature lunar landscape. The background dissolves into a beautiful, soft, dark charcoal-gray bokeh of volcanic stone with out-of-focus golden light leaks. The artist name "Pürhā" is floating ethereally in the blurry background, made of semi-transparent particles of golden dust glowing softly in the bokeh, barely legible.
