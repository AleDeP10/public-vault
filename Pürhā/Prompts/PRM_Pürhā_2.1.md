A perfect 1:1 square aspect ratio photographic album cover composition. An impossible surreal tropical night scene. A hybrid surreal object where a classical acoustic guitar is morphing and fusing with a massive colonial iron cannon. From the cannon barrel, lush palm leaves, piano keys, and glowing green guitar-string vines sprout directly from the rust. The background features an intense indigo and magenta sky, with a phosphorescent neon-cyan ocean. The guitar wood glows from within, emitting a warm amber and liquid-gold light through its soundhole, casting long, liquid-purple shadows on an old stone wall. The artist name "Pürhā" is floating in the air, emerging from the cannon's mouth like semi-transparent, bioluminescent UV-light smoke, with glowing, curving letters dissolving into the atmosphere. Lisergic, hypnotic, psychedelic prog-rock aesthetic.
