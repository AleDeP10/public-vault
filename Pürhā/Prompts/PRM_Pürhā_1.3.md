A perfect 1:1 square aspect ratio photographic album cover composition. A classical acoustic guitar leans against a massive, deeply corroded colonial iron cannon on an ancient stone wall. Tropical beach background with an intense, blinding ultramarine blue ocean and vibrant, neon-green palm leaves. Sharp harsh noon tropical sunlight, deep black shadows, high contrast. Every single detail is hyper-defined: the cannon's rust shows bleeding red layers, orange flakes, and deep craters. The guitar wood looks exposed and raw under the bright sun. The artist name "Pürhā" is aggressively carved and scratched directly into the scarred metal of the cannon, the deep cuts catching sharp white sunlight reflections like sharp blades. Intense, raw, urgent, aggressive and powerful atmosphere.
