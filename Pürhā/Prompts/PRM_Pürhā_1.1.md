A perfect 1:1 square aspect ratio photographic album cover composition. A classical acoustic guitar is leaning against a massive, heavily rusted colonial iron cannon. The scene is set on an ancient stone rampart overlooking a calm tropical ocean. Lush palm trees and tropical greenery visible behind the low stone wall. Nostalgic 1970s analog film aesthetic, visible light film grain, soft vignetting, subtle chromatic aberration. Golden hour lighting coming from the side, long warm shadows, making the guitar's spruce wood glow like liquid honey with copper grain. The cannon's rust has rich terracotta and burnt ochre textures. The ocean is a soft, watercolor-like petrol blue. The artist name "Pürhā" is deeply scratched and carved directly into the rough, rusty metal of the cannon barrel, with irregular, handmade letters revealing lighter raw iron underneath, looking like an old soldier's graffiti. Moody, nostalgic, melancholic atmosphere.
