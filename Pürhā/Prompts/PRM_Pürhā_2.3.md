A perfect 1:1 square aspect ratio photographic album cover composition. Conceptual art style. The background is an endless, borderless, clean fog of pearl-gray and ash-blue, where the tropical sea and sky blend into total emptiness. In the center, a hybrid surreal sculpture of a classical acoustic guitar fused into a heavily rusted iron cannon stands alone. A single, dramatic, theatrical white spotlight cuts vertically from above, illuminating the object and casting intense, micro-detailed shadows on its raw textures of oxidized bronze rust and split wood grain. The artist name "Pürhā" is floating ethereally above the sculpture, written in soft milk-white letters that are gently blurring and evaporating into the surrounding mist. Poignant, lonely, museum installation aesthetic, melancholic silence.
