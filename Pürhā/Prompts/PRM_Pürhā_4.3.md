A top-down, perfectly centered overhead photograph for a 1:1 square aspect ratio album cover. Zen conceptual art style. The composition is a vast, minimal canvas of pristine, untouched, fine tropical white sand with subtle cream and ivory micro-textures. In the center, a dark, jagged, organic fissure tears through the sand. Sprouting directly from this dark crack, the wooden headstock of an acoustic guitar emerges, its broken strings twisting and seamlessly melting into the rusted iron barrel of a small, semi-buried colonial cannon. Clean, clinical zenith lighting from directly above, eliminating long shadows. The artist name "Pürhā" floats precisely above the crack, written in wavy, semi-transparent letters that ripple like a delicate heat haze rising from the ground.
